Platform

What is DrugMind and how does it work?

+

DrugMind is a multi-agent AI platform for drug discovery that accelerates the journey from target identification to lead optimization. It combines four specialized AI agents working in concert:

  • Molecular Generator — creates novel drug-like molecules using diffusion models and reinforcement learning
  • Docking Agent — performs GPU-accelerated molecular docking against protein targets
  • ADMET Predictor — evaluates absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion, and toxicity properties
  • Lead Optimizer — iteratively refines candidates to balance potency, selectivity, and drug-likeness

The platform has analyzed 10,847+ molecules and maintains 95% binding accuracy against benchmark datasets.

What types of drug targets does DrugMind support?

+

DrugMind supports a wide range of target types:

  • Kinases — EGFR, BRAF, ALK, and 200+ kinase targets
  • GPCRs — serotonin, dopamine, adrenergic receptors
  • Nuclear receptors — estrogen, androgen, PPARs
  • Protein-protein interactions — PD-1/PD-L1, MDM2/p53
  • Enzymes — proteases, phosphatases, epigenetic targets
  • Ion channels — sodium, potassium, calcium channels

You can also upload custom PDB structures for novel or proprietary targets.

What is the Lipinski Rule of Five and does DrugMind enforce it?

+

Lipinski's Rule of Five is a set of guidelines for predicting drug-likeness: molecular weight ≤500 Da, LogP ≤5, H-bond donors ≤5, H-bond acceptors ≤10. DrugMind provides real-time Lipinski compliance checking during the design process.

You can choose to strictly enforce Lipinski rules, apply them as soft constraints, or ignore them entirely for specialized use cases (e.g., PROTACs, macrocycles, or peptide therapeutics that often violate traditional rules).

Technical

What AI models power DrugMind?

+

DrugMind uses a combination of state-of-the-art models:

  • Molecular generation — diffusion-based generative models (similar to DiffusionDB) fine-tuned on ChEMBL and ZINC datasets
  • Molecular docking — GPU-accelerated AutoDock Vina with custom scoring functions
  • ADMET prediction — graph neural networks trained on 2M+ experimental ADMET data points
  • Lead optimization — multi-objective reinforcement learning with Pareto optimization

All models are regularly retrained with new data and benchmarked against public datasets (MoleculeNet, PDBbind).

How fast is DrugMind's virtual screening?

+

DrugMind achieves 100x speedup over traditional docking methods:

  • Screen 1 million compounds against a single target in ~2 hours (GPU cluster)
  • ADMET prediction for 10,000 compounds in ~5 minutes
  • Full lead optimization cycle (generation → screening → optimization) in ~30 minutes

Exact speed depends on your plan tier and target complexity.

Security & Compliance

How does DrugMind protect my proprietary molecular data?

+

DrugMind takes molecular data security seriously:

  • End-to-end encryption — AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit
  • No model training on user molecules — your structures are never used to train our AI
  • Tenant isolation — each organization's molecular library is completely isolated
  • Audit logging — full traceability of who accessed what data and when
  • SOC 2 Type II certified infrastructure
  • Enterprise on-premise deployment available for maximum data control

Is DrugMind GDPR compliant?

+

Yes. DrugMind is fully GDPR compliant with:

  • Data Processing Agreements (DPA) available for all EU customers
  • EU data center option (Frankfurt) for data residency requirements
  • Right to erasure — complete data deletion within 30 days of request
  • Data portability — export all your data in standard formats at any time

Can I upload my own molecular libraries?

+

Yes. Researcher plan and above support uploading custom molecular libraries via:

Uploaded data is private to your account and never shared with other users.
