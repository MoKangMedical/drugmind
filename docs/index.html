Core Capabilities

From molecular design to clinical trials, AI covers every stage of drug R&D

Target Discovery

Knowledge graph-based target identification, integrating genomics, proteomics, and literature mining. Druggability scoring and priority ranking with GWAS and Mendelian randomization.

Molecular Design

AI-driven molecule generation (VAE, GAN, diffusion), structure-based virtual screening, lead optimization with multi-objective ADMET constraints, and drug-likeness assessment.

ADMET Prediction

Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion, Toxicity — comprehensive pharmacokinetic profiling with RDKit-powered analysis and Lipinski compliance checking.

Clinical Trial Simulation

Virtual patient generation, endpoint prediction, statistical power analysis, adaptive trial design optimization — simulate before you invest.

Real-World Data

EMR integration, insurance claims analysis, real-world evidence generation, pharmacovigilance monitoring — from trial to market.

Team Collaboration

Multi-agent structured discussions, consensus formation, decision logging with full audit trail — your virtual R&D team never sleeps.

FAQ

What is DrugMind?
DrugMind is an AI-powered drug R&D digital twin collaboration platform. It deploys 5 specialized AI agents (Medicinal Chemist, Biologist, Pharmacologist, Data Scientist, Project Lead) to analyze drug discovery questions from every professional angle, 24/7.
How does the digital twin work?
Each AI agent is trained on domain-specific knowledge and follows role-specific reasoning patterns. They engage in structured discussions, debate trade-offs, and reach consensus — simulating a real R&D team's decision-making process.
What data sources are supported?
ChEMBL, OpenTargets, UniProt, PubMed, ClinicalTrials.gov, PubChem, and more. The platform also supports private data integration for Enterprise plans.
How is DrugMind different from MediPharma?
MediPharma is a tool-chain (execution + computation). DrugMind is a collaboration platform (decision + coordination). MediPharma's APIs can be embedded in DrugMind — they are complementary, not competing.
